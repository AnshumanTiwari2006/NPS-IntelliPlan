      Glossary of Institutional Terms

      Current Age

      Your current age based on official documents. NPS entry is allowed from 18 to 70 years.

      Retirement Age

      The age at which you plan to stop contributing and start your pension. Usually 60, but can be extended up to 75.

      Monthly Contribution

      The amount you invest in your NPS Tier-I account every month. Minimum â‚¹500/month required.

      Risk Profile

      Conservative: 25% Equity max. Moderate: 50% Equity. Aggressive: Up to 75% Equity. Higher equity usually leads to higher long-term returns but with more volatility.

      Annual Step-Up

      The percentage by which you increase your monthly contribution every year (e.g., to match salary hikes). This is one of the most powerful ways to build a massive corpus.

      Employer Contribution

      Monthly contribution made by your employer. Under Sec 80CCD(2), this is tax-deductible up to 10% of your Basic + DA.

      Inflation Rate

      The expected annual rise in cost of living. A 6% inflation means â‚¹1 Lakh today will only buy â‚¹18k worth of goods in 30 years. Our tool uses this to show your "Real Purchasing Power".

      Target Monthly Pension

      The amount of monthly income you want in retirement. Note: If you enter â‚¹50,000, the tool checks if your *nominal* future pension meets this, but warns you about the inflation gap.
